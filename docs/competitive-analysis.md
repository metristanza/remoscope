## Executive Summary

The on-demand field verification market is established but fragmented, with several players serving different verticals. The three primary competitors—WeGoLook, ProxyPics, and iVueit—all share a common limitation: **they deliver asynchronous reports after the fact, not real-time interactive video.**

Remoscope's core differentiator is **live, directed video streaming**, enabling instant decision-making that no competitor currently offers. This positions Remoscope to disrupt the market by solving the "I need to see it NOW" problem that existing solutions don't address.

## Key Market Insights

### 1. The "Async Gap"

All three competitors deliver **asynchronous verification**—photos and reports that arrive after the agent visit. None offer the ability to:

- See the property **live** as it's being inspected
- **Direct the agent** in real-time ("show me the roof", "pan left")
- Make **instant decisions** based on what you're seeing

This is Remoscope's primary market opportunity.

### 2. Enterprise Lock-In

Competitors have focused heavily on enterprise clients (insurance companies, large lenders, national retailers). This leaves the **SMB market underserved**:

- Small real estate investors
- Remote cleaning/service operators
- Independent property managers
- Event planners
- Local news organizations

### 3. Agent Quality vs. Quantity

ProxyPics has the largest network (395K) but pays the lowest for basic tasks ($3.25). WeGoLook pays better but has slow payment processing (10+ days). 

**Opportunity:** Offer competitive pay with fast payment to attract higher-quality agents.

### 4. Vertical Concentration

| Competitor | Primary Vertical | % of Business (est.) |
|------------|------------------|----------------------|
| WeGoLook | Insurance | 70%+ |
| ProxyPics | Lending/Mortgage | 60%+ |
| iVueit | Facilities Management | 50%+ |

All competitors are concentrated in 1-2 verticals, creating opportunity for a **multi-vertical disruptor**.

## Threats to Monitor

### 1. Competitor Response

ProxyPics or WeGoLook could add live video capabilities. However:
- Technical debt makes this difficult
- Would cannibalize existing async business
- Takes 12-18 months to build and deploy

### 2. New Entrants

- Tech giants (Google, Amazon) could enter with drone/video solutions
- Telecom companies could leverage existing infrastructure
- Security camera companies could pivot

### 3. Market Education

Buyers may not know they need live video until they try it. Marketing must:
- Highlight async limitations
- Demonstrate real-time value
- Create urgency around decision-making

## Conclusion

The on-demand field verification market has established players but a clear technological gap: **no one offers live, interactive video streaming**. 

WeGoLook owns insurance. ProxyPics owns lending. iVueit owns facilities. But no one owns **real-time verification**.

Remoscope's opportunity is to define and dominate this new category before competitors adapt.

**Key strategic imperatives:**

1. **Own "live verification"** as a category
2. **Start with underserved verticals** (remote work, SMB real estate, events)
3. **Build agent network** with competitive pay and fast payment
4. **Maintain SMB focus** while competitors chase enterprise
5. **Move fast** — first-mover advantage is critical
